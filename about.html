Why We Started

The Gap No One Was Filling

Most AI tools are built for developers. ChatGPT, Copilot, and the thousands of AI products that followed. powerful in a vacuum, but generic. They don't know what a CAPA is. They don't know why DPD buckets matter. They don't understand the difference between how a loan gets qualified at an NBFC versus a bank.

Industry operators. the people running manufacturing plants, growing edtech platforms, managing NBFC loan books. know their workflows in extraordinary detail. They know exactly which problems cost the most, which tasks eat the most hours, and where the biggest inefficiencies hide. What they lack is a way to turn that knowledge into working AI, without hiring an ML team or waiting 9 months for a custom build.

That's the gap Upcore fills. We translate operational expertise into deployed AI agents. domain-trained, human-supervised, and live in days rather than quarters.

Generic AI tools
Powerful but context-blind. Requires months of prompting and domain knowledge transfer that most teams don't have the bandwidth for.
Custom ML development

What We Believe

The Convictions We Build By

01
Industry context is the moat, not model size.
A generic AI that needs you to explain what "CAPA" or "DPD bucket" means is not a production tool. it's a prototype. Industry-specific agents already know your terminology, your edge cases, and your regulatory environment. That depth of context is what separates useful AI from impressive demos.
02
Control builds trust. Trust enables scale.
Every Upcore agent operates within guardrails you define. Nothing executes at scale without your approval where it matters. We believe the organisations that will get the most from AI aren't those who deploy the most autonomously. they're the ones who deploy with the most intentional oversight. Trust is not an obstacle to AI adoption; it's the prerequisite for it.
03
Speed to deploy is a competitive advantage.
The window between identifying an operational problem and having an AI solution for it should be measured in days, not quarters. Every month an operator waits for a custom build, a competitor is moving. We believe deployment speed is not just a technical property. it's a strategic one. 72 hours is not a marketing claim; it's an architecture decision.
04
Automation should amplify people, not erase them.
The best AI implementations we've seen don't eliminate teams. they make teams dramatically more effective. Agents handle the repetitive, high-volume, time-sensitive work. Humans handle judgment, relationships, and exceptions. This division of labour isn't a compromise. it's the only model that produces reliable outcomes in complex operational environments.
